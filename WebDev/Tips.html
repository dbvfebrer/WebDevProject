Kitchen Secrets



Life Lesson from the Kitchen



  • It teaches you to conquer fear
    For kitchen virgins, fear is a big hindrance. The fear of splattering hot oil, of cutting oneself, of burning what is being cooked, or even being judged for what one cooks prevent a lot of people from learning to cook. But in cooking and in life, conquering your fears is the first step to getting things done and starting new adventure.
  • It teaches you respect and humility
    No matter how good you are, you can only cook within the confines of natural law. You cannot command a pot to boil on a whim. It is dependent on the flame's heat and the water's boiling point. You become a cook by learning to flow in the kitchen, by feeling your way around it. Mastering the elements happens by respecting its nature and using it to your advantage as a cook. You will also learn to respect your own limitations and learn to cook based on your own capacity.
  • It teaches you to be disciplined and to be patient
    When you cook, there are things you can do, but there are things only time can do. So when cooking, there are things you work for and some things you wait for, like when you have to cook the meat until tender. Good food cannot be rushed. Just like developing your skills. It takes time and effort.
  • It teaches you to plan and to organize
    The success of your cooking greatly depends on how well you plan and organize not only your kitchen but also your ingredients and the cooking process itself. This is why mise en place is a cornerstone of a cook's kitchen practice, not to mention what planning and organizing can do for your budget. So when you make it part of the way you live, it can have a poweeful impact.
  • It teaches you to clean up
    When you work in the kitchen, you amass a pile of garbage and a load of dishes, pots and pans to wash, which can amount to alot if you don't clean up as you go along. Cooking trains you to go after yourself, which may also serve you well in other aspects of your life. When you move ahead without obstruction, you can flow.
  • It teaches you to focus and to take your mind away from negative vibe.
    Cooking has both therapeutic and spiritual benefits. To cook well, one has to be focused and act towards cooking something delicious. No one intentionally cooks to make something bad or awful. It's againsr the nature of the act, which is grounded on love and service.

  • It teaches you to do your best

    When you cook, you rarely do it for yourself. You cook for other people, most often the people you care about and love. When you learn to cook, you are motivated to aspire to become better every time. And because it is such a selfless activity, you cannot help but want to do your best all the time. You become better in process and continue to raise the bar for your personal best.
  • It teaches you to let go. Cooking teaches you impermanence
    It teaches you to detach from things that are fleeting, such as praise and criticism. Your cooking, no matter how perfect, will be consumed in minutes, and criticisms, when they do come, will be forgotten when you are able to redeem yourself with the next meal you prepare. So, take in criticism graciously and use it to furhter improve your cooking. There is always room for improvement.
  • It teaches you to find peace in yourself
    If you love cooking, it makes you happy and feel fulfilled with what you do. And that gives you satisfaction enough to be at peace with yourself. That is all that matters. And you will cook because you love to cook.
  • It teaches you to love
    When you immerse yourself in the selfless act of cooking, you put your ego aside and cook out of service and generosity. You become a kinder person and make loving easier by cooking for others.


What should I cook?


Identify the main ingredients.

Determine what main ingredients to use in the recipe. Sometimes, it is meat; at other times, it can be seafood, vegetables or noodles. What is used in each recipe automatically becomes the main ingredient.

Choose the cooking method or methods.

Develop the idea of the cook and determine the appropriate way to cook a dish based on what you want to do. The second element is the method of cooking This is what you will think of when you already have the basic ingredients. The cooking method must be appropriate to the main ingredients because each ingredients has it own charcteristics and ideal cooking method for better taste. Sometimes, a combination of cooking methods is needed, and certain preparations have to be done before the actual cooking can start.

Shape the flavor with condiments, ingredients, and sauces.

This is where the taste and style of the dish begin to develop. The taste and character of the dish can vary from here. Simple chicken seasoned with salt, garlic, and pepper will become a deifferent sih when garlic is replaced with star anise or rosemary. From the Filipino flavor, it can taste Chinese or European by simply changing a few ingredients, but the main ingredients and cooking method will still be same. This ie where the recipe ends. Or you can continue innovating depending on your taste preference.

Add special ingredients, if desired.

To make chicken taste awesome, for example, you add special ingredients to it: potatoes, carrots, and raisins first; pineapple and boiled egg in the second; and olives and chorizo in the third. They can also be rotated to vary the outcome. But if you look, only one system is followed based on the anatomy of recipe.

Garnish and serve with sauce or side dish.

And to finish the dish: First, serve it with fish sauce and lime; second, add some boiled spinach; and third, place chopped parsley on top. Simple decoration, siding, or garnish gives the dish a unique feel.


MAKE THE CUT

Make your knife cut as a knife should


Be sharp about knives

Be sharp with your blade: how to care for your sharp blade and sharp knife.

Bread Knives

Bread knives are designed for slicing different types of bread. Most bread knives feature offset handles, which are designed to prevent the user's knuckles from hitting the cutting board. Additionally, you can usually find bread knives between 7 to 10 inches long.

Cleavers

With a wide and long blade, cleavers are some of the largest knives a chef will have in his/her cutlery kit. Despite their size, cleavers feature thin and lightweight blades that can be used for number of tasks, such as crushing garlic, chopping vegetables, and slicing meat.

Boning Knives

Featuring flexible, semi-flexible, or stiff options, boning knives allow users to separate meat from bones while reducing the amount of wasted meat.

Chef's Knives

You can find chef's knives in nearly every commercial kitchen, and they are versatile tools that can be used for variety of cutting tasks. Chef's knives have a wide blade that tapers to a point. This point is suitable for a number of prep tasks, such as chopping, mincing, and slicing.

Paring Knives

This extremely versatile type of knife can be used for many food prep tasks from peeling vegetables and chopping fruits to deveining shrimps and slicing cheese.


A knife of good quality will be useful for a long time.

Choose one that has a firm grip in your hand and has a sturdy handle. Once you have found one that suits you, learn to keep it in good shape all the time.

Gear Up: Know your pots and pans

Stockpot

The stockpot is a large and deep cooker with a flat bottom. It is used in cooking soup, broth, and pasta. It is not used in frying.

Fry Pan/Skillet

The frying pan or skillet has a flat botom rounded edges. Perfect for quick cooking high heat, such as cooking simple stew searing, so it has no cover.

Saucepan

It has a rounded bottom and tall, straight sides. it has many uses, such as cooking sauces and broths, boiling eggs, deep-frying, or heating food, so it is a favorite tool of people with small kitchens.

Saute Pan

It is suitable for stewing. You can mix food in it and simmer the food until cooked.

Casserole/Braisng Pan

The casserole nas a flat and thick bottom. It is used to soften meats that need long cooking, such as estofado and morcon. You can also stew or sear meat here before serving, it is also usually made of stainless steel or oven_proof materials so that it can be inserted fully into the oven.

Pressure Cooker

A pressure cooker is a stove where you can control the presure of the heat inside it to facilitate the cooking of your food. With safety valve, pressure and heat are trapped inside the caserola to cook the food to a tender doneness.
